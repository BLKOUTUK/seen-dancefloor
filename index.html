01 — what it is

A two-hour live broadcast: an ensemble chat show about the Black queer dancefloor — its music, its history, what it has meant in Black queer life across the diaspora.

02 — the form

Nobody leaves the stage. The night is a conversation — and the performances grow out of it.

/ 01

Guests stay

Our guests are on stage the whole evening — not a turn and a goodbye. Chat, stories, memory, disagreement.

/ 02

Two hosts, paired

The deskbound geek — the stats, the cultural-memory depth — paired with a younger, fresher voice. Depth and energy, holding the thread between them.

/ 03

Segments surface

A performance, a teaching moment, a debate, a reading — each grows out of the talk, not scheduled against it.

/ 04

It ends as a floor

The conversation is the spine. The closing set turns the broadcast into a dancefloor.

casting The younger host is cast through an open audition — a way in for new voices, and the search itself runs as the campaign: hype that builds the audience before the broadcast.

the staging — the room the night is held in

Not a club, not a stage to be performed at — a warm room lined with records, a host at the counter, the guests gathered round. You are welcomed in.

03 — the crate · eight records

The crate sets the agenda — eight records, the spine of the night.

Artists are invited to respond to any or all of it. The core audience comes informed, having seen the crate already. The two hosts guide the night through it — conversation and audience segments, built from the crate's topics. Options, not a running order.

01Joyful

The Floor Opens

Disco, soul, the floor at full celebration. The night lifts off here.

02Peak

Brian Green

The footwork sleeve. A dancer walks the room through the basics — house, footwork, the steps that built the night.

03Light

Lil Nas X / Little Richard

The Versus sleeve. A comedy debate with a real argument inside it — who owns the lineage, who got paid.

04Depth

Tongues Untied

After Marlon Riggs, 1989. His words on anger — unvented, becoming — given a body and a voice. The temperature drops, on purpose.

05Tender

Looking for Langston

After Isaac Julien, 1989. One voice and a guitar over Julien's images. The room goes quiet for Langston.

06Breath

Les Ballets Nègres

Berto Pasuka's company, 1946. The quiz — eighty years of Black dance, Pasuka to a TikTok step from this week.

07Turn

Is Dancehall Ready?

Dancehall reclaimed. A music weaponised against queer life, taken back and danced on queer terms — live.

08Close

Paradise Garage

Larry Levan's floor. The closing set — disco, house, the lineage to now. The broadcast becomes a dancefloor.
